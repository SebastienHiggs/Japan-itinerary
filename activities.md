## Osaka
**23–25 Apr · 2 nights · The OneFive Osaka Namba Dotonbori**

> **Timing:** Arrive ~7:50pm Apr 23 — evening only. One full day Apr 24. Check out by 10am Apr 25, then shinkansen to Okayama. Tight — prioritise ruthlessly.

### Notes
- 

### Recommended
**Highlights:**
- Dotonbori — neon canal strip, 5 min walk from hotel. Perfect for arrival evening
- Kuromon Ichiba Market — Osaka's kitchen; great for breakfast or lunch on Apr 24
- Shinsekai — retro neighbourhood, old-school kushikatsu bars
- Hozenji Yokocho — narrow stone laneway, moss-covered shrine, atmospheric at night
- Osaka Castle + park — worth a look from outside even if skipping the interior

**Food:**
- Takoyaki (octopus balls) — eat them on the street in Dotonbori
- Okonomiyaki — Osaka-style savoury pancake
- Kushikatsu in Shinsekai — deep-fried skewers, the local specialty
- **Wagyu/Kobe beef** — Osaka is the better base for this. Kobe is 30 min away by shinkansen; plenty of excellent Kobe beef restaurants in Osaka itself. This is the right city for the fancy wagyu meal.

**For Sebastien:**
- Den Den Town (Nipponbashi) — Osaka's electronics district, ~15 min from hotel. Retro games, components, hobby electronics. Good warm-up before Akihabara in Tokyo.

## Kyoto
**27 Apr – 1 May · 4 nights · Hotel Tou Nishinotoin Kyoto**

> **Timing:** Arrive afternoon Apr 27. Four full days Apr 28–30 + May 1 morning (check out by 11am, shinkansen to Tokyo). Golden Week starts Apr 29 — Fushimi Inari, Arashiyama, Kiyomizudera will be very crowded from then. Hit the busiest spots on Apr 28 or go very early morning.

### Notes
- 

### Recommended
**Highlights:**
- Higashiyama & Kiyomizu-dera — do this Apr 28 before Golden Week crowds hit
- Arashiyama bamboo grove — go early morning (7am) even during Golden Week
- Fushimi Inari (early morning) — iconic torii gates; perfectly manageable at sunrise
- Gion evening streets — atmospheric any evening, less crowded after 8pm
- Sagano Torokko Scenic Train — vintage open-air train through the Hozu River gorge from Saga-Torokko to Kameoka; one of the best scenic rides in Japan. Book in advance, sells out during Golden Week. Pairs perfectly with the Hozugawa boat ride (see Sebastien's section).
- Philosopher's Path — canal-side walking path, lovely in spring

**Food:**
- Kyoto set meals / kaiseki-style
- Tempura
- Nishiki Market snacks — narrow covered market, great for grazing

### Phoebe wants to do
**UJI DAY TRIP** *(~30 min from Kyoto by JR Nara Line or Kintetsu — easy half-day or full day)*
- Byōdō-in Temple — stunning, on the 10-yen coin
- Riverside walks
- Matcha desserts
- Green tea soba

**In Kyoto:**
- Katayama Bunzaburo (gift store)
- Kahitsukan (gallery)
- Amam Dacotan (bakery)
- Ryouen (specialty tea place)
- Angers (home goods)
- Hotel Tou Nishinotoin — Phoebe chose this hotel; worth exploring the property itself
- Eight (bakery)
- Kikuo Shoten (print store)

### Sebastien wants to do
- **Hozugawa River Boat Ride** (保津川下り) — 2-hour ride down the Hozu River rapids from Kameoka to Arashiyama. Scenic gorge, actual rapids, great fun. Pairs perfectly with the Sagano Torokko train (take the train one way, ride the boat back). **Book in advance.**
- Sagano Torokko Scenic Train — see Recommended above; do this as a combo with the boat ride

> **Day planning note:** The Hozugawa/Torokko combo is part of an Arashiyama day, not a separate trip — so realistically you have 3 full days: one with Phoebe in Uji, one Arashiyama day (with boat + train), one free for the rest of Kyoto.

## Hakone
**3–4 May · 1 night overnight from Tokyo**

> **Timing:** Travel from Tokyo ~1.5 hrs (Romancecar from Shinjuku is the scenic option, ~85 min). Return to Tokyo May 4. Golden Week — book accommodation and Romancecar well in advance.

### Notes
- Staying with friends — personal preferences less relevant
- **Mt Fuji is the goal here** — best views are from Lake Ashi and the Hakone Ropeway on clear days. May can be hit or miss with cloud cover; fingers crossed.

### Recommended
- **Hakone Ropeway** — rides over Owakudani volcanic valley with Mt Fuji views on clear days
- **Lake Ashi cruise** — boat across the lake with iconic Mt Fuji backdrop (postcard views)
- **Owakudani** — active volcanic area, bubbling sulphur vents, black eggs boiled in volcanic water
- **Hakone Open Air Museum** — excellent outdoor sculpture park; genuinely worth it even in rain
- **Onsen** — hot spring bathing is the whole point of Hakone; most ryokans include it
- **Romancecar train** — scenic limited express from Shinjuku; book seats in advance

## Kuala Lumpur
**8–10 May · 3 nights · JW Marriott Kuala Lumpur**

> **Timing:** Arrive 6:10am May 8 (overnight flight from Tokyo). Check out by noon May 10, fly home at 9:15am — so effectively May 8 afternoon + full day May 9 + May 10 morning. ~2.5 days. The JW Marriott is in Bukit Bintang — central location, walkable to a lot.

### Notes
- 

### Recommended
**Highlights:**
- **Petronas Twin Towers** — iconic; worth going up (Skybridge + observation deck). Book online in advance.
- **Batu Caves** — Hindu temple built into dramatic limestone cliffs, 30 min from city. Go early to avoid heat.
- **Bukit Bintang** — the neighbourhood you're staying in; great food, night markets, walkable
- **Jalan Alor** — famous street food strip a short walk from the hotel; best at night
- **KLCC Park** — free, lovely walk around the base of the towers with fountains
- **Bangsar** — upscale neighbourhood, good cafes and restaurants; ~15 min Grab ride
- **Chinatown (Petaling Street)** — busy market street, worth an hour

**Food:**
- Nasi lemak — the national dish, eaten for breakfast
- Char kway teow — wok-fried flat noodles, smoky and rich
- Roti canai — flaky flatbread with curry dipping sauce; everywhere, very cheap
- Laksa — spicy coconut noodle soup
- Cendol — shaved ice dessert, great in the heat
- Dim sum — KL has excellent Cantonese dim sum (good for the morning of May 8 after landing)
