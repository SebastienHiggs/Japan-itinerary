### Notes
- 

### Recommended
**Highlights:**

### Recommended

## Hakone
**3–4 May · 1 night overnight from Tokyo**

> **Timing:** Travel from Tokyo ~1.5 hrs (Romancecar from Shinjuku is the scenic option, ~85 min). Return to Tokyo May 4. Golden Week — book accommodation and Romancecar well in advance.

### Notes
- Staying with friends — personal preferences less relevant
- **Mt Fuji is the goal here** — best views are from Lake Ashi and the Hakone Ropeway on clear days. May can be hit or miss with cloud cover; fingers crossed.

### Recommended
- **Hakone Ropeway** — rides over Owakudani volcanic valley with Mt Fuji views on clear days
- **Lake Ashi cruise** — boat across the lake with iconic Mt Fuji backdrop (postcard views)
- **Owakudani** — active volcanic area, bubbling sulphur vents, black eggs boiled in volcanic water
- **Hakone Open Air Museum** — excellent outdoor sculpture park; genuinely worth it even in rain
- **Onsen** — hot spring bathing is the whole point of Hakone; most ryokans include it
- **Romancecar train** — scenic limited express from Shinjuku; book seats in advance

**8–10 May · 3 nights · JW Marriott Kuala Lumpur**

> **Timing:** Arrive 6:10am May 8 — rest up, afternoon/evening only. One full day May 9. Fly home May 10 at 9:15am meaning leaving the hotel around 6am. Effectively **1.5 days**. The JW Marriott is in Bukit Bintang — central, walkable to most things.
>
> **Rough shape:** May 8 evening → Jalan Alor night market (walkable from hotel). May 9 → Batu Caves in the morning, Petronas Towers afternoon/evening.

### Notes
- 

### Recommended
**Highlights:**
- **Petronas Twin Towers** — iconic; worth going up (Skybridge + observation deck). Book online in advance.
- **Batu Caves** — Hindu temple built into dramatic limestone cliffs, 30 min from city. Go early to avoid heat.
- **Bukit Bintang** — the neighbourhood you're staying in; great food, night markets, walkable
- **Jalan Alor** — famous street food strip a short walk from the hotel; best at night
- **KLCC Park** — free, lovely walk around the base of the towers with fountains
- **Bangsar** — upscale neighbourhood, good cafes and restaurants; ~15 min Grab ride
- **Chinatown (Petaling Street)** — busy market street, worth an hour

**Food:**
- Nasi lemak — the national dish, eaten for breakfast
- Char kway teow — wok-fried flat noodles, smoky and rich
- Roti canai — flaky flatbread with curry dipping sauce; everywhere, very cheap
- Laksa — spicy coconut noodle soup
- Cendol — shaved ice dessert, great in the heat
- Dim sum — KL has excellent Cantonese dim sum (good for the morning of May 8 after landing)
